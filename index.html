What are the problems with Districts?

If districts are so great, why aren't all FRC competitions Districts?

  • More liability
    Districts have traditionally required that a region form a non profit to assume all responsibility for the events, a so called 'Program Delivery Organization'. This is a lot of work, and transfers liability from FIRST to the regional volunteers. However, starting in 2026, California will be pioneering a model that does not require a PDO. We are watching that with great interest.
  • More work
    Districts require nearly doubling the number of events held each year. That is a lot of work, and requires a lot of additional volunteers, particularly key volunteers such as FTAs, head refs, and volunteer coordinators.
  • More fundraising
    Districts in PDOs have to perform substantial fund raising. While the overall economics of districts are favorable, to get them up and running requires that regional volunteers raise and sustain dedicated funds for the process.
  • Smaller events
    Districts are generally smaller events, perhaps held in a high school gym. This is in contrast to current Minnesota regionals which are held in impressive venues such as the DECC or Mariucci. They also have reduced sound and light. The overall feel of a district event is not as impressive as one of our typical Regionals. The District Championship event will remain an inspiring large scale event.

About Us

Our Mission

Grassroots Northland is a group of individuals working to improve the experiences of FRC teams in MN, ND, & SD. Our efforts include working to improve region sustainability, team experiences, and advocating for more regional competitions with a long-term transition plan to districts.
