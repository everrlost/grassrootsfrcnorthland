What are Districts?

This video explains how districts work, and why they are the better option for FRC Northland.

Here is FIRST's official description and our list of reasons to prefer districts:

  • More plays
    For the same $6,000 fee that buys you one regional with at most 9 matches, you get two events, each with 12 matches. That's roughly 2.5x times more matches for the same money.
  • More cost effective
    District events can cost substantially less than regional events. That allows a regions fundraising dollars to go further, potentially funding other initiatives instead of investing all the money in large venues.
  • More local
    District events are usually closer to teams, reducing travel time and costs for most teams.
  • Less time
    District events are only two days, not three. The impact on student homework and missed class time is much less significant.
  • More champs bids
    Minnesota and the Dakotas have historically been under represented at Champs. A district system would guarantee that our region sends a proportional number of our teams to the world championship. The new regional qualification system may be a partial solution to this problem, but districts would ensure fair representation.

What are the challenges with Districts?

If districts are so great, why aren't all FRC competitions Districts?

  • More work
    Districts require nearly doubling the number of events held each year. That is a lot of work, and requires a lot of additional volunteers, particularly key volunteers such as FTAs, head refs, and volunteer coordinators.
  • Smaller events
    Districts are generally smaller events, perhaps held in a high school gym. This is in contrast to current Northland regionals which are held in larger venues such as the DECC or Mariucci Arena. The District Championship event will remain an event with a high production value.
  • Possible issues with fundraising and liability
    Prior to the announcement of districts for California, districts required independent fundraising and the formation of a non profit which could assume liability for the events. The California districts are a new model, backed by FIRST, that may no longer have those challenges. If the Northland uses this new model, this may not be a signficant challenge.

About Grassroots Northland

Grassroots Northland is a group of individuals working to improve the experiences of FRC teams in MN, ND, & SD. Our efforts include working to improve region sustainability, team experiences, and advocating for more regional competitions with a long-term transition plan to districts.

Take our survey!

Our current effort is to educate teams and to learn more about team's thoughts on districts. If you could take this short survey, we would appreciate it. Our plan is to collate the data and make it available to the broader Northland community.
